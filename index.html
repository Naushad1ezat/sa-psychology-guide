Understanding Psychology in South Africa

What is Psychology?

Psychology is the scientific study of human behaviour and mental processes. It incorporates multiple dimensions, including behavioural, social, biological, and cognitive sciences, with an emphasis on human experience in individual and environmental contexts.

In South Africa, psychology is situated within the Faculty of Health Sciences and is influenced by a variety of academic disciplines such as sociology, philosophy, and cultural anthropology.

Goals of Psychology

  • Describe: Observe and detail behaviour and mental processes.
  • Understand: Explore underlying causes and explanations.
  • Predict: Forecast future behaviour based on understanding.
  • Control: Influence and modify behaviour to improve lives.

Major Subdivisions of Psychology

This section covers key areas such as abnormal, cognitive, developmental, health, and forensic psychology, each contributing to our understanding of behaviour in different contexts.

  • Abnormal psychology focuses on psychological disorders and other abnormal psychological phenomena. Psychologists investigate the nature, incidence, causes, treatment, and prevention of psychological disorders, aiming to understand and alleviate mental illness.

  • This branch studies mental processes such as sensing, perceiving, remembering, forgetting, thinking, decision-making, reasoning, and problem-solving. Cognitive psychologists explore how information is presented and processed in the mind, providing insight into how the mind works.

  • Community psychology emphasizes social factors affecting mental health, focusing on prevention and community interventions rather than treatment. It explores social change, advocacy, and empowerment within diverse communities.

Other Psychology Categories

Psychology encompasses a variety of fields including clinical, developmental, health, educational, forensic, and social psychology. Each category addresses specific aspects of behaviour and mental processes.

Category Description
Clinical Psychology Focuses on diagnosing and treating mental disorders.
Educational Psychology Studies how people learn and improve educational processes.
Health Psychology Explores psychological influences on physical health.
Forensic Psychology Applies psychology to legal issues and the justice system.

Comparing Related Disciplines

Psychology, psychiatry, and psychotherapy are related but distinct disciplines:

  • Psychiatry: A medical field focusing on diagnosing and treating mental illness using medication and therapy.
  • Psychotherapy: A therapeutic process to improve mental health and emotional wellbeing.
  • Psychology: The scientific study of behaviour and mental processes, often involving assessment and counselling.
