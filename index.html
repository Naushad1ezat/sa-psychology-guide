Understanding Psychology in South Africa

What is Psychology?

Psychology is the scientific study of human behaviour and mental processes. It incorporates multiple dimensions, including behavioural, social, biological, and cognitive sciences, with an emphasis on human experience in individual and environmental contexts.

In South Africa, psychology is situated within the Faculty of Health Sciences and is influenced by a variety of academic disciplines such as sociology, philosophy, and cultural anthropology.

Goals of Psychology

  • Describe: Observe and detail behaviour and mental processes.
  • Understand: Explore underlying causes and explanations.
  • Predict: Forecast future behaviour based on understanding.
  • Control: Influence and modify behaviour to improve lives.

Major Subdivisions of Psychology

This section covers key areas such as abnormal, cognitive, developmental, health, and forensic psychology, each contributing to our understanding of behaviour in different contexts.

  • Abnormal Psychology: Study of psychological disorders and treatments.
  • Cognitive Psychology: Study of memory, problem-solving, and perception.
  • Community Psychology: Focus on mental health within community settings.
  • Developmental Psychology: Study of life-span changes in humans.
  • Environmental Psychology: Study of how environments influence behaviour.
  • Forensic Psychology: Applying psychology to law and justice.
  • Health Psychology: Promoting well-being and managing illness.
  • Neuropsychology: Exploring the brain’s effect on behaviour.
  • Sport Psychology: Enhancing athletic performance.

Registration Categories in South Africa

South African psychologists must register with the HPCSA under one of several categories.

  • Clinical Psychologist: Diagnoses and treats mental health issues.
  • Counselling Psychologist: Helps individuals adjust and cope.
  • Educational Psychologist: Focus on learners and development.
  • Forensic Psychologist: Works in legal settings.
  • Industrial/Organisational Psychologist: Workplace behaviour specialist.
  • Neuropsychologist: Assesses and rehabilitates brain function.
  • Research Psychologist: Conducts studies on human behaviour.

Visit the HPCSA website to learn more.
